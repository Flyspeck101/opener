9% Cannon

TD/C-Spin Opener

9% Cannon is a TD opener which has a good 8-height PC chance. It was first documented here as a TD opener with only a 1/11 chance of having to stack a compromise setup (hence 9% Cannon), without being given a proper name. (No, I will not accept 1/11th compromise cannon as a proper name.)

The first bag can be built 50% of the time, 60% including mirror.

Second Bag

Usually the second bag is built as the completion and execution of the TD. There are two main routes.

Route 1 (100% cover)

This setup is always possible with 180 spins. If you don’t have 180 spins, then this opener isn’t really for you.

Route 2 (66.67% cover, L -> S)

This setup offers the best PC chance.

However if they are really mean the player may also opt for a centre 4-wide setup, with 6 residue:

Route 1 Third Bag

Assuming that the player went with the first route, they will be left with this residue:

This residue has a 96.87% PC chance, which rises to 100% if the T piece is one of the first three pieces in the third bag. The I piece is fixed at the top-left. Most of the solutions have the T piece in the T-Spin slot and a double heart pattern above. Example:

There are 14 minimals.

If you can’t do the PC or you received garbage, just take the T-Spin and continue with 4-5 stacking. STSD or DT Cannon setups are also possible.

Route 2 Third Bag

If the player chose to take the second route, the will be left with this residue:

This shape has a 98.57% PC chance, with 24 minimals. These solutions aren’t as straightforward as Route 1 but are still generally easy.

If the Perfect Clear isn’t possible, there are a range of continuations. However, note that the overstacked left side makes downstacking much harder, so it’s better to just freestyle. Generally, Route 2 is better for Blitz/Ultra runs since it is much more difficult to continue if there’s no PC.

Notes

  • While I independently discovered this opener, I was not the first to find it, so I probably do not really have the right to give this opener a name.

  • However, all of the sources I uncovered gave the name “A gun that can only be compromised by subtracting 1/11” (which effectively means “1/11 compromise chance cannon”). 1/11 = 9.09090909090909…%
  • They also didn’t specify any PC solutions that I could see.
